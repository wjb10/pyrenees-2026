Where we stay

Five bases. One night in Luchon to land, three in Luz on the French side (the hiking heartland), one over the border in Torla for Ordesa, then the coast. Late June is pre-peak and pre-Tour, so prices are normal, but book San Sebastián and the Torla night early.

Logistics

🚗 The drive

  • Car: pick up at Toulouse-Blagnac on arrival, drop on departure. Confirm cross-border cover for Spain at the desk (you cross twice: Bielsa tunnel in, the coast out).
  • Bielsa tunnel (Day 5): open year-round, single-tube, 60 km/h. Up the Vallée d'Aure past Saint-Lary, then through to Spain.
  • Final morning: Biarritz → Toulouse airport is ~3h. Leave the coast by 10:30 for a late-afternoon flight.

📅 Book ahead

  • San Sebastián hotel (fills earliest)
  • Torla night for Ordesa (small village, books up)
  • Auberge des Aryelets, Aulon (Day 2 lunch, Bib Gourmand)
  • Elkano, Getaria: call weeks out (Tue–Sat 10:00–17:00)
  • Pic du Midi cable car: online, pick a clear day
  • Surf lesson, Côte des Basques

🥾 Ordesa, the one rule

  • Private cars are banned in the valley 19 Jun–20 Sept, so park in Torla and take the shuttle into the Pradera (every ~20 min).
  • Capacity caps at 1,800, so start early; the shuttle stops when full.
  • On Day 6 you hike, then drive ~3h to San Sebastián. A 3–4h route (up to the Gradas de Soaso) keeps the day sane; the full Cola de Caballo loop is 6h.

🌦 Late-June notes & kit

  • High cols (Tourmalet, Aspin) reliably open; pack a fleece, the Pic du Midi is cold even in summer.
  • The Brèche de Roland and high Néouvielle still hold snow into July: check before going high.
  • Hiking shoes, swimmers (Cauterets baths, San Seb + Biarritz beaches), offline maps for the valleys, cash for the Torla shuttle and small cafés.
